Maharaja Agrasen College, affiliated with Delhi University, is spread across a 10-acre campus
in Vasundhara Enclave, East Delhi. It is conveniently located near the New Ashok Nagar,
Noida Sector 15, and Trilokpuri metro stations, ensuring easy accessibility, making it well-
connected to various parts of the city and beyond.
Metro: Maharaja Agrasen College is conveniently located near three metro stations—New
Ashok Nagar, Noida Sector 15, and Trilokpuri. From any of these metro stations, you can
easily reach the college by taking an e-rickshaw, which typically costs just ₹ 20. This is a quick
and affordable way to commute, ensuring a hassle-free journey to and from the campus.
Bus: The college is well-connected by several bus routes that service the area. Nearby bus
stops include Vasundhara Enclave and Noida Mod. These stops offer frequent services to
various parts of Delhi and Noida, making it convenient for students and visitors. For detailed
bus routes and schedules, you can visit the DTC (Delhi Transport Corporation) website.
Auto-Rickshaws and Taxis: The area around Maharaja Agrasen College is well-serviced by
auto-rickshaws and taxis. Common ride-hailing apps like Ola and Uber are widely available,
providing easy and efficient transportation options for those traveling to and from the
campus.
Whether you're using public transport or a ride-hailing app, getting to Maharaja Agrasen
College is straightforward and affordable, with multiple convenient options available.

ACCOMMODATION GUIDE
Finding the right accommodation is essential for a comfortable and successful college life.
Here’s a guide to help you navigate your options:
On-Campus Accommodation
Maharaja Agrasen College offers an on-campus Girls' Hostel with a limited capacity. If you are
a female student, you can apply for accommodation here. The hostel provides a safe and
secure environment with essential amenities like meals, Wi-Fi, study areas, and common
rooms. However, due to the limited number of seats, it’s advisable to apply as early as
possible.
Off-Campus Accommodation Options
For students who do not get a place in the on-campus hostel or prefer to stay off-campus,
there are several other accommodation options available:
Paying Guest (PG) Accommodations:1.
Must-Have Amenities: Ensure that the PG offers basic amenities such as a bed, wardrobe,
Wi-Fi, and study table. Look for additional facilities like laundry services, daily meals, and
regular cleaning.
Ventilation: Proper ventilation is crucial for a healthy living environment. Avoid PGs with
rooms in the basement as they can be damp, poorly lit, and poorly ventilated.
Food Quality: Good nutrition is key to maintaining your health while you focus on your
studies. Check if the PG provides fresh, hygienic meals regularly. Ask for a trial meal
before finalizing your stay.
No Lock-In Period: Be cautious about lock-in periods in rental agreements. Ideally, opt for
accommodations that offer flexibility without binding you to a long-term commitment.
This allows you to move out if you find better options or face any issues.
   2. Flats and Apartments:
Location: Look for flats that are close to the college or well-connected via public
transportation, such as metro stations or bus stops. Proximity to markets, pharmacies, and
other essential services is also important.
Amenities: Ensure the flat is equipped with basic furnishings, appliances (like a stove,
refrigerator, and washing machine), and reliable internet access. If you are sharing, check if
there is enough space and privacy for all occupants.
Safety and Security: Make sure the building has proper security measures, like CCTV,
gated entry, and a security guard. Always prioritize your safety, especially if you’re living
away from home for the first time.
Ventilation and Natural Light: Flats with good ventilation and ample natural light
contribute to a healthier and more pleasant living environment. Avoid places that are
cramped or poorly lit.
--- PAGE 4 ---
   • No Lock-In Period: Just like with PGs, avoid flats with restrictive lock-in periods.
Flexibility is important, especially if you’re new to the city and still getting acquainted with
different areas.
Must-Bring Amenities
When moving into your new accommodation, make sure to bring the following essential
items to make your stay more comfortable:
Bedding: Bring your own bedding, including bedsheets, pillows, and blankets. Hostel beds
might not be very comfortable, so consider bringing a mattress topper for added comfort.
Kitchen Essentials: If your accommodation allows cooking, bring basic utensils, a kettle,
and some quick snacks to start with. A small portable induction cooktop can be handy for
quick meals.
Personal Care Items: Stock up on toiletries, towels, laundry detergent, and cleaning
supplies. A first-aid kit with basic medicines is also a good idea.
Study Materials: Bring your own study lamp, stationery, and a comfortable chair if your
room doesn’t provide one.
Storage Solutions: Carry extra storage boxes or organizers to keep your belongings tidy
and maximize space in smaller rooms.
Electrical Appliances: Consider bringing essential electrical items like a power strip, a fan,
and a small fridge (if not provided) to store food and drinks.
Emergency Supplies: A torch, batteries, and a portable charger can be lifesavers during
power outages or emergencies.
General Tips for Accommodation
Proximity to Campus: Staying close to the college can save time and commuting costs.
Look for areas around New Ashok Nagar, New kondli, Mayur Vihar Phase 3, Noida Sector
15, or Trilokpuri metro stations as they are easily accessible to the college.
Shared vs. Individual Accommodation: Decide whether you prefer living alone or with
roommates. Sharing can reduce costs, but it’s important to find like-minded people to
avoid conflicts.
Inspect Before You Rent: Always visit the accommodation before making a decision.
Check the condition of the rooms, washrooms, kitchen, and common areas.
Contract and Rent Agreement: Ensure you have a proper rent agreement in place that
clearly states the rent, deposit, and terms of stay. Be cautious of hidden charges or unfair
clauses.
Finding the right accommodation might take some time, but it’s worth the effort to ensure a
comfortable and conducive environment for your studies and well-being. Take the time to
explore your options, ask for recommendations from seniors, and make an informed
decision.

ELECTIVE SUBJECTS
Our college follows the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, which introduces several
elective subjects to enhance your academic journey. Here’s a brief overview:
1. Skill Enhancement Course (SEC)
SEC focuses on developing technical and practical skills, essential for your future career.
Across your 6 semesters, you'll choose different SECs, each designed to enhance specific
skill sets. These courses are tailored to make you industry-ready and proficient in
various practical domains.
2. Value Addition Course (VAC)
VACs are offered in the first 4 semesters and are aimed at building your moral and
ethical foundation. These courses focus on holistic development, teaching values that are
crucial in personal and professional life.
3. Generic Elective (GE)
GEs are available primarily for honors students, allowing you to pursue a subject that
interests you outside of your major. Once you choose a GE, it remains consistent
throughout your degree, eventually becoming your minor specialization. For BAP
students, you have the option to choose Hindi or English as your GE.
4. Ability Enhancement Course (AEC)
AECs are designed to enhance your abilities in specific areas. You’ll study Environmental
Studies (EVS) in one year and an Indian language in another, ensuring a well-rounded
education. This sequence is designed to repeat in different years to ensure
comprehensive learning
.
Additional Information:
SEC and VAC Selection: You’ll receive a list of available courses via email from the
college. Make sure to choose courses that align with your interests and career goals.
Further Details: More detailed information can be found in the college prospectus
available on the college website. Additionally, these details will be discussed during
the college orientation session.

As a BAP student, you will have a unique structure where you choose a combination of
two subjects. Each year, you’ll select one of these subjects as your major, while the other
becomes your minor. You have the flexibility to change your major and minor subjects
in 3rd Semester based on your interests or academic goals.
How It Works:
Major Subject: You will study both the major and minor papers for your major subject.
This means more in-depth learning and focus on the subject you've chosen as your
major for that year.
Minor Subject: For your minor subject, you will only study the minor paper, giving you
a broader understanding of this secondary area.
Example Combination:
Let’s say you choose a combination of History and Political Science:
Year 1: If you choose History as your major, you will study both major and minor papers
in History, and only the minor paper in Political Science.
Year 2: You may switch and make Political Science your major, meaning you'll now
study both major and minor papers in Political Science, and only the minor paper in
History.
Building Your Degree:
If you decide to maintain History as your major subject throughout all your years, and
meet the minimum required credits, History will be recognized as your main degree,
with Political Science as your minor. This combination will be reflected in your final
degree, showing your specialization in History along with a minor in Political Science.

Maharaja Agrasen College, University of Delhi, aims to foster an environment of mutual
respect, academic excellence, and personal growth. The following Code of Conduct is
designed to ensure that all students contribute to a positive and productive campus
atmosphere.
1. Academic Integrity
Honesty: Students are expected to uphold the highest standards of honesty and integrity
in all academic work, including assignments, examinations, and projects. Plagiarism,
cheating, or any form of academic dishonesty is strictly prohibited.
Original Work: All work submitted by students should be their own. Proper citations must
be provided when referencing others' work, ideas, or research.
2. Respect for Others
Diversity and Inclusion: Respect for all individuals, regardless of their background,
religion, gender, sexual orientation, or ethnicity, is mandatory. Discrimination,
harassment, or bullying in any form will not be tolerated.
Civility: Students should engage in respectful dialogue with faculty, staff, and fellow
students. Abusive language, threats, or intimidation are unacceptable.
Classroom Conduct: Attend all classes regularly and participate actively. Refrain from
disruptive behavior, such as using mobile phones or talking during lectures. Be punctual
and come prepared.
3. Campus Behavior
Cleanliness: Keep the campus clean. Dispose of litter in the designated bins, and maintain
cleanliness in classrooms, common areas, and hostels.
Use of College Property: Respect and care for college property, including furniture,
equipment, and facilities. Vandalism or misuse of college property will result in
disciplinary action.
4. Hostel Conduct
Curfew: Adhere to the hostel curfew timings. Late-night entries should be avoided unless
prior permission is granted.
Visitors: Follow the hostel rules regarding visitors. Unauthorized visitors are not allowed
in the hostel premises.
5. Safety and Security
Identification: Always carry your college ID card while on campus. Present it when
requested by college authorities.
Substance Abuse: The use of drugs, alcohol, or any other intoxicants is strictly prohibited
on campus and in college-hosted events.
