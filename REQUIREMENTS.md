---

## 1. Purpose & Vision

Replace the static 2024 PDF pocket guide with a fast, mobile-first web app that a fresher can open on their phone during admission season and their first weeks at MAC. It should answer the questions freshers actually have — how do I reach campus, where do I live, who do I call, which society do I join, how do electives work — in the fewest possible taps.

It is a *pocket guide*, not a website: short, scannable, always at hand, shareable as a single link (and QR code on posters/admission desks).

### Goals

- Every fresher can find any answer in under 3 taps.
- Loads fast on cheap phones and slow campus/metro internet.
- Feels hand-made by seniors for juniors — warm, personal, a little desi — **not** like a generic AI/template site.
- Easy for the union to update names/numbers each year without redesigning.

### Non-Goals

- No login, no accounts, no database of students.
- Not a replacement for the official college website or DU portals.
- No admission form processing — only guidance and contacts.

---

## 2. Audience & Context of Use

| Who | Situation | What they need |
|---|---|---|
| Incoming fresher (17–19) | On phone, possibly mid-commute or standing in an admission queue | Directions, contacts, quick answers |
| Parent/guardian | Helping with admission & accommodation | Safety info, hostel/PG guidance, grievance contacts |
| Senior/volunteer | Helping a fresher | A link to share instantly on WhatsApp |

**Device reality:** 90%+ mobile, mostly Android, often on 4G with patchy signal. Desktop is secondary but must not break.

## 4. Functional Requirements

- **FR1 — Navigation:** Sticky bottom navigation (mobile) with 4–5 top destinations + a full menu. Every section reachable in ≤3 taps from home.
- **FR2 — Search:** Simple client-side search across sections, societies, and contacts.
- **FR3 — One-tap actions:** All phone numbers are `tel:` links, emails are `mailto:`, Instagram handles deep-link to the app, address opens Google Maps.
- **FR4 — Share:** Native share button (Web Share API) on every section; WhatsApp-friendly link previews (proper OG title/description/image per section).
- **FR5 — Offline:** Works after first load with no network (service worker cache). Freshers will open it in the metro.
- **FR6 — Installable:** PWA — "Add to Home Screen" with proper icon and name ("MAC Pocket Guide").
- **FR7 — Collapsible content:** Long sections (accommodation, code of conduct) use accordions; nothing requires reading a full page to find one line.
- **FR8 — Society filter:** Category chips + text filter on the societies list.
- **FR9 — Language touch:** Interface in English; sprinkle Hindi/Hinglish in headings and micro-copy where natural (see §6). Optional stretch: full Hindi toggle.
- **FR10 — Updatable data:** All names, numbers, and society lists live in one data file (JSON/YAML), separate from layout, so next year's union edits one file.
- **FR11 — QR entry:** Stable URL suitable for QR codes printed on admission-desk standees and the physical guide.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance:** < 100 KB critical payload; interactive in < 3 s on a mid-range Android over 4G. No heavy frameworks required — static site or lightweight SPA. Lighthouse mobile score ≥ 90.
- **Compatibility:** Chrome/WebView on Android (primary), Safari iOS, and functional on desktop. Works down to 360 px width.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA contrast, ≥ 16 px body text, 44 px tap targets, alt text on images, semantic headings, no information conveyed by colour alone.
- **Reliability:** Static hosting (no server dependency) — Replit static deploy, GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Vercel. No downtime during admission rush.
- **Privacy:** No analytics that require consent banners; at most privacy-friendly counting (e.g., Plausible/GoatCounter). No personal data collected from visitors.
- **Cost:** ₹0 hosting target (free tiers). Optional custom subdomain if college provides one.

## 6. Design Requirements — "Not designed by AI"

The single most common tell of AI-generated sites: purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism cards, emoji-as-icons, generic hero with centered headline + two buttons, Inter font everywhere, uniform rounded cards in a 3-column grid, and corporate copy ("Empowering students to excel"). **All of these are banned.**

Instead:

- **Voice first, decoration second.** Copy written by actual seniors, first-person plural ("We've all haggled with the e-rickshaw bhaiya — it's ₹20, don't pay more"). Keep the practical, slightly protective tone of the 2024 guide. Micro-doses of Hinglish where it lands naturally.
- **Colour:** Derive the palette from college/union identity (e.g., the maroon/saffron/navy family used in MAC + ABVP material), plus paper-white backgrounds. One accent colour, used sparingly. No gradients as decoration.
- **Typography:** A characterful display face for headings (e.g., a slab/serif or a Devanagari-friendly pairing) + a clean body face. Not Inter-on-white default.
- **Texture & imperfection:** Design nods to a real pocket booklet — page-like sections, a torn-paper or notebook motif, hand-drawn arrows/underlines/doodles, sticker-style badges for society categories, real photos of campus and the union team (even phone-shot ones — real beats stock).
- **Icons:** One consistent icon set OR small hand-drawn icons. Never emojis as UI icons.
- **Layout:** Asymmetry is fine. Vary section rhythms — a checklist, then a card list, then a plain prose note — instead of identical card grids repeated 10 times.
- **Motion:** Minimal. No scroll-triggered fade-in-up on every element. At most one signature touch (e.g., page-flip feel between sections).
- **Details that feel human:** hand-written style "tips from seniors" callouts, a "made with ❤️ by your seniors — Student Union 2026 × ABVP" footer, credit the actual people who built it by name.
